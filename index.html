1887

A gramophone is a vintage sound-reproducing machine that uses a hand-cranked mechanism and a needle to play shellac disc records, typically at 78 RPM, producing sound acoustically through a horn. Patented by Emile Berliner in 1887, these devices revolutionized audio playback before the electric era.

The radio was invented in the mid-1890s, with Guglielmo Marconi widely recognized for developing the first practical radio system and receiving the first patent for wireless telegraphy in 1896. While Marconi is credited with the invention, Nikola Tesla demonstrated wireless communication earlier in 1893, and key, long-distance transmission, and voice broadcasting were pioneered by others.

1963

The radio cassette player revolutionized portable audio after its introduction by Philips in 1963. Originally designed for dictation, it gained massive popularity as a "2-in-1" device in the 1970s, enabling users to play tapes and record radio. Its peak came in the 1980s with portable players like the Sony Walkman, dominating until CDs and digital players emerged in the 1990s and 2000s.

The first smartphone, the IBM Simon Personal Communicator, was invented in 1992 and released in 1994, featuring a touchscreen, email, and PDA capabilities. It evolved from 1980s "brick" phones into modern pocket computers, with key milestones including the 2007 iPhone revolutionizing touchscreen interfaces and the 2008 launch of Android.
